# Week 3: Control of Ventilation, Ventilation-Perfusion Matching, Cardiovascular Responses

This week covers the neural and chemical control of ventilation during exercise, ventilation-perfusion matching in the lung, and the cardiovascular responses that deliver oxygen to working muscles.

## Lectures

- [**Lecture 7: Control of Ventilation and Ventilation-Perfusion Matching**](lecture07-ventilation-perfusion-matching) (27 slides)
   Control of ventilation by brainstem centers and chemoreceptors, ventilatory response curves to CO<sub>2</sub> and O<sub>2</sub>, the Fick principle applied to blood gas transport, ventilation-perfusion ratio (V/Q), shunts, dead space ventilation, and V/Q heterogeneity across species.

- [**Lecture 8: Cardiovascular Responses to Exercise**](lecture08-cardiovascular-responses) (39 slides)
   The Fick principle for O<sub>2</sub> delivery, heart rate and stroke volume responses, Frank-Starling mechanism, blood pressure regulation, blood flow redistribution, oxygen-hemoglobin dissociation and the Bohr effect, cardiovascular drift.

- [**Week 3 Friday Review and Discussion**](friday-review) (20 slides)
   Review and discussion session consolidating Week 3 material: V/Q matching and heterogeneity, the oxygen-hemoglobin dissociation curve (Bohr effect, T/R conformations), myoglobin function, the graphical solution to the Fick principle, a think-pair-share on how physiological factors map onto the Fick equation, and a practice problem calculating cardiac output and stroke volume.
