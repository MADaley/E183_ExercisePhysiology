# Week 3: Control of Ventilation, Comparative Pulmonary Function, Cardiovascular Responses

This week covers the neural and chemical control of ventilation during exercise, compares gas-exchange architectures across vertebrate groups, and introduces the cardiovascular responses that deliver oxygen to working muscles.

## Lectures

1. [**Ventilation-Perfusion Matching and Comparative Pulmonary Function**](lecture06-ventilation-perfusion-comparative) (45 slides)
   Control of ventilation by brainstem centers and chemoreceptors, ventilatory response curves, comparative gas-exchange systems (mammalian tidal, avian crosscurrent, fish countercurrent).

2. [**Cardiovascular Responses to Exercise**](lecture07-cardiovascular-responses) (43 slides)
   The Fick principle for O<sub>2</sub> delivery, heart rate and stroke volume responses, Frank-Starling mechanism, blood pressure regulation, blood flow redistribution, oxygen-hemoglobin dissociation and the Bohr effect, cardiovascular drift.
